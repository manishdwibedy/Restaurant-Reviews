Dark, rustic, post modern, funky, slightly eccentric in decor, and LOUD, Gjelina seems to fit in well with the eclectic, &quot;do your own thing&quot; attitude that is Venice. I went to the Sony Studios in Culver City with friends, but since I was so close to Venice, I made my excuse to go solo to Gjelina which I have been wanting to go for a long time. I got there about 6pm on a Friday night so parking and getting into the restaurant was no problem. But I would not go there close to 8pm and expect the same kind of luck.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nI had a really great waiter named OZZY who is really awesome. He knows his wines and knows what kind of dish pairs with the beers and wines. And most of all he is very helpful and kind. If you go, make sure to ask for OZZY. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nI ordered the Wood Roasted Peel &amp; Eat Shrimp which was redolent with the mellow, pleasantly stinky garlic, pico de gallo (red chile powder), and lemon. The the whole shrimps were fresh and sweet with a slight sea flavor, and I loved sucking out the juices in the head of the shrimps. There aren't many shrimps since this is a tapas, but I enjoyed every one of them. Ozzy suggested pairing a Phillipe Rimbaud, '10 Sauvignon Blanc from Sancerre in the Loire Valley region which had slightly more minerality and less grassiness &amp; citrus notes than the typical Sauvignon Blanc from better known Bordeaux.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nI then ordered the Crispy Veal Sweet Breads which was complemented by earthy, meaty Shiitake mushrooms, capers, and a nice, slightly sweet Madeira Demi Glace sauce. The sweet bread had a wonderful mouth feel, slightly grainy with a fresh, subtle, oily meaty taste. Since I could only drink two glasses of wine, asked Ozzy to find me a wine that would fit both the Crispy Veal and the final dish. I was suggested the Sanzay Cabernet Franc from the Loire region. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nI finally got the Pizza with Lamb Sausage, Confit of Tomato, Rapini, Pecorino &amp; Asiago cheese. The pizza here is rather thin like a Tarte Alsacienne. The lamb sausage was slighty gamy, and the tomato with the cheeses was strong enough in flavor to compete with the funky little lamb. The Sanzay Cabernet Franc also was strong enough in tanin and acid along with a herbal spiciness to contrast well and modulate the gaminess of the lamb.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nDessert was Affogato with Caramel and Biscotti. The ice cream was drowned in a bit of dark, rich, bitter espesso. This was a fantastic conclusion to a great meal suggested and approved by Ozzy. I left Gjelina totally satisfied and sated. I love the food here which is not fussy or contrived, but I am sorry that I live so far away. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nBy the way, I would have given 4 1/2 stars, but Ozzy's great service and hospitality upped it to 5 stars. (Interestingly, many of the servers here sported a Chignon. Is this the new trend happening in Venice? Vive la Difference!)\n                                        <div class=\"clear\"></div>\n                </div>\n                <div class=\"rev-text-sm mbot0\">\n                    <div tabindex=\"0\" class=\"rev-text-expand \" style=\"line-height: 20px;\">\n                        \n                        &quot;Rustic&quot; Fare. Are you ready for a scathing review. I am wondering if the people who have given this place rave reviews really do have taste buds or just want everyone to know they are on the cool train on the way to hype. And no this is not authentic tapas. Just small versions of European dishes that fail to wow. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nIf you have ever been to Europe or live there this place is going to utterly disappoint. The service was good, 2 star instead of 1, but the food was bland if not plain boring. With such lovely ingredients I just don't see how it can be possible. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe dish I was most excited about, as I have tried this recipe and failed, was no improvement on my attempt. The quail with pomegranite sauce was more akin to bland chicken with a jar of tomatilla. There wasn't the remotest hint of the sweet savory this bird needs or the pomegranite suggests. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nArtisan cheeses-misnomer. Blue cheese and a run of the mill goat cheese do not qualify. Neither does a sweaty piece of gruyere or whatever it was-again nondescript. The cheese with the sage undertones was lovely, but not enough to wow when partied with grocery store cheeses. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe lamb chops were acceptable while the mint sauce was so so. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nNot even the Burratta with grilled nectarines were able to wow. Were the nectarines even ripe? And where were the contrasting flavors that this dish requires to bring out the flavor of the cheese. Same for the grilled nectarine and ice cream dessert. On this dish they got the ice cream and pistachio right at least. Sweet and salt contrast was lovely- chef more of that. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe Japanese red Snapper failed to arrive as they had ran out. Not a big fan of food running out. Although fresh fish is more understandable. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe pizza across from us smelled good but until I taste it I am not putting my signature on anything. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nWhile at a spot up the road waiting for our table a man sitting next to us at the bar said something very telling about this place. &quot;It's...interesting. Whatever they did to hype it certainly worked.&quot; Operative word being hype. Not one word about the actual food or oh try this dish. I don't go to restaurants to pay 15 dollars a dish to be a part of the hype. I go to restaurants period to experience wonderful and exciting flavors. So unless being seen in the right spot is your first priority spend your money elsewhere this place is just too full of itself.\n                                        <div class=\"clear\"></div>\n                </div>\n                <div class=\"rev-text-sm mbot0\">\n                    <div tabindex=\"0\" class=\"rev-text-expand \" style=\"line-height: 20px;\">\n                        \n                        OVERRATED!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!.
