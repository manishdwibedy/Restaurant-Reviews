bizarre. Jose Andres' restaurant sits on the cusp of Beverly Hills and Tron. With the different areas of the restaurant (bar, patisserie, dining rooms and, of course, the obligatory consignment / pawn area) it feels sort of like a foreign but familiar world. The restaurant still felt comfortable, but with just enough of intrigue to keep your head on a swivel to see who you might see or who might want to see you. Communal tables, great view of the kitchen and a lot of great food porn and people watching are just the beginning.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nFirst thing's first: cocktails. The &quot;Liquid Cherry&quot; Manhattan offers your choice of some form of whiskey with a 'cherry spherification.' Of late, rye has been the drink of choice, so the Manhattan was made with Michter's Small Batch rye. The came and was finished before the signature cocktail, the LN2 Caipirinha, was finally made. What's a LN2 Caipirinha? In lay terms, that's cachaca, fresh lime and sugar frozen with liquid nitrogen and turned into a boozy slush right in front of your eyes. However, the novelty of a table side science experiment cocktail is a strong draw (yes, even for me too), and it took almost 20 minutes before the drink cart made it's way over to our table. Both drinks were (OBVIOUSLY) nice and cold and refreshing. Was it worth the wait? Maybe next time we'll opt for a quicker drink, but it's worth doing at least once.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nAfter the modern, updated version of &quot;molecular gastronomy,&quot; the meal shifted gears to old-fashioned molecular gastronomy: the cheese plate. Manchego 'pasamontes' sheep's milk cheese, a creamy cow and goat Valdeon blue and a wood-smoked, sheep's milk Idiazabal. The cheeses were presented on a slate serving dish with almonds and a quince jam. Each cheese was distinct, well crafted and savory. The quince jam was a perfectly subtle sweetness to balance out and accompany the cheeses.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nRojo y Blanca's menu is divided into two halves: traditional tapas and modern tapas. The cheese was as traditional the meal got. There are few things that are better than a good foie gras. One of them is on this menu. Foie gras on a stick. Foie gras on a stick delicately wrapped in cotton candy. I'm not sure which episode of &quot;The Best Thing I Ever Ate&quot; this could fit into, but it's on my list. If I got up and left after this foie-lly pop, I'd have been content. Probably not full, but content. Boneless chicken wings, smoked yellowtail, and saut&eacute;ed cauliflower &quot;cous cous&quot; all followed. They were all great dishes. The yellowtail was delicately smoked, not like my nemesis, smoked salmon, and was delicious. Smoked any-fish makes me nervous, but one taste of the fresh yellowtail had me sold. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nStill not full and fully convinced by the man and his precocious 11 yo daughter sitting next to (with) us, we ordered one last dish: Not You're Everyday Caprese. Cherry tomatoes, liquid bocconcini (mozzarella balls) and fresh basil leaves. The liquid mozz balls were definitely different. Like when you reach for your glass thinking it's just water only to be surprised that it's carbonated. It looks like a mozz ball, tastes like a mozz ball, but has a much thinner, liquidy texture. This was a good surprise and a good recommendation by our new friends.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nAside from the sloooow start with the caipirinha, the service was top notch. The server was friendly and very knowledgeable about the menu. No question was too difficult nor were the answers farther away than the tip of his tongue. Waters were refilled without asking, second cocktails were offered at the right time and everything was right where we needed it before we even knew we needed it.
