I went in November for my birthday for lunch and the food was exquisite. Four of us went and had the prix fixe lunch. I wasn't feeling very well on my drive over from the valley and it was too late to change plans. I was very worried that I wouldn't be able to eat anything, so i chose my menu carefully. I had a Beet Salad to start, Risotto for my main course and the Grapefruit Confit for dessert. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe beet salad was refreshing and light, a little sweet, a little tangy and with the cheese managed to be a little creamy. After a few bites of my salad I felt about 70% better than when I arrived.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe Risotto was the best I've ever had. The rice itself was perfectly cooked and the combination of the zucchini, squash and cheese melts in your mouth until you smile.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe Grapefruit Confit was the biggest surprise, it came in a martini glass and it's the most unique dessert I've ever tried that I truly loved. It had grapefruit confit (preserved ruby grapefruit) with grapefruit sherbert over that and a meringue to top it off. The taste combination was incredible. Light, sweet, a little sour and 100% refreshing. By the time I finished my dessert I had forgotten that I had even felt sick at all that day. I don't know if its the miracle of Ramsay's cuisine or what but that was a seriously great meal.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nI've been to Gordon Ramsay's flagship restaurant on Royal Hospital Road in London and to Gordon Ramsay at the London in New York. and just by comparison the Los Angeles restaurant is much more casual (for lunch), it reflects the laid back California lifestyle and I really like how unique it is to LA and unique even to Ramsay's other restaurants.
