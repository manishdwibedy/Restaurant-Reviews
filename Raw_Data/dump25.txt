Finally made it out to osteria mozza this saturday to indulge in italian eats. we decided on a late dinner so i grabbed a ressie at 9:15pm. since i wanted to check out the place, i arrived at o'mozza at 8:30 and hung out at the bar with a couple friends as we soaked in the atmosphere. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nwhile at the bar, we wanted a light wine to serve as an aperitif and the bartender suggested the roero arneis, bruno giacosa. this was an extremely light and refreshing wine and the acidity woke my mouth up for some eats.<br />\r\n<br />\r\neveryone arrived in time for our ressie but the restaurant was backed up with folks hanging out at the tables so our ressie was pushed back 40+ mins. it would have been nice for the hostesss to come by the bar to tell us that our seating time would be pushed back.....unfortunately i had to make my way through the throngs of people to ask her when they planned on seating us. bummer.....anyhoo, we killed time chatting and sipping wine at the bar.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nwe were finally seated around 10pm at a round table in the side room. some people have complained about the side room...calling it the kiddie table.....but i found this room to be better than the main dining area. reason being? well......the noise was not deafening so the six of us could hold conversations without yelling.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nwhile we perused the menu, our wine was exhausted so we ordered the following for dinner:<br />\r\n<br />\r\n- barbera d'alba sorito mosconi, rocche dei manzoni: this was a soft red that paired well with the anti-pasti's<br />\r\n- i had my heart set on a barolo because i wanted a big red, however, the sommelier was adamant about getting another red (i don't remember the name....doh!) that was 1/2 the price of the barolo i wanted. so much for standard upselling by sommeliers! cheers to the sommelier because this red was fantastic after it was allowed to breathe and open up for 30-40mins.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nfor the six of us, we ordered the following to share family style:<br />\r\n<br />\r\nanti-pasti<br />\r\n- grilled octopus: doooooood! yum! the octopus was oh so tender, it was like biting into a marshmallow. there was a nice smokey flavor imparted by the grill as well. the citrus-y salad really went well with the octopus.<br />\r\n- burrata w/ bacon: the creamy burrata was heavenly. i seriously could have subsided on just the burrata by itself. however, paired with the bacon and seasonings, this dish was very well balanced.<br />\r\n- burricoti w/ artichokes, pine nuts and olive oil: i hate pine nuts, but i enjoyed this dish. dunno who came up with the combo, but it worked out very nicely.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nprimi<br />\r\n- gnocchi with wild boar ragu: OMG!! the gnocchi was oh so fluffy and soft and the wild boar ragu was slightly gamey (which i like), yet subtle. weird huh? the sauce was a tad salty by itself but paired with the gnocchi, it was good. i'd go back just for a bowl of their gnocchi....no sauce needed. hehe!<br />\r\n- garganelli: this is a good ole pasta with bologneses sauce. the pasta was a perfect al dente and the bolognese sauce is probably in the top three i have had...to date.<br />\r\n- orechiette: as touted by folks, the dish as a whole was darn good. the orechiette was a perfect al dente and when paired with the sauce made for a fantastic dish. however, i found the sausage/chard sauce to be lil too greasy and salty by itself.<br />\r\n<br />\r\neveryone got their own entree and this is how it broke down:<br />\r\n<br />\r\nsecondi's<br />\r\n- guinea fowl crostone: this was what i ordered and man was i thrilled. i've never had guinea fowl before and this dish was a party in my mouth. the guinea fowl was cooked perfectly and was extremely juicy. the meat was a tad on the gamey side which is the reason why i ordered the dish . the sauce was a hearty liver pancetta concoction that was finger licking good.<br />\r\n- grilled beef tagliata: three folks ordered this dish and i had a sample. the beef is definitely a high quality cut as it was very tender and beefy. i really loved my taste of this simple dish.<br />\r\n- grilled whole orlata: my friend to my left ordered this dish and the fish was cooked perfectly. however, the skin was burnt in places so the presentation was kinda funky. 3&quot;x3&quot; patches of black char here and there..ugh. the orlata was stuffed with leafy greens which may have helped maintain the juiciness of the flesh. <br />\r\n- wild striped bass: the last person ordered the striped bass. dunno why...it's quite a mundane dish to begin with. kinda like ordering salmon. but what the hey. for $28, he definitely did not get his money's worth as the piece of fish was maybe 3.5oz's on a good day. 4 bites and it was gone. i didn't try this dish so i have no further comment.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nservice was extremely attentive all night and the manager came by a handful of times to check in on our group as well. dishes were cleared promptly, wines were topped off automatically and new place settings materialized quickly.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nosteria mozza is no babbo, but it is good italian in LA.<br />\r\n<br />\r\np.s. there's a lot of street parking on highland starting 1.5blocks south of o'mozza.\n                                        <div class=\"clear\"></div>\n
