We headed out to Venice for a nice dinner and an evening on the beach. Too cold for the beach, but this place made the drive worth it. <br />\r\n<br />\r\nAs soon as we were seated they brought us rolls and a bottle of chianti, though we declined the wine. The garlic-encrusted bread rolls were excellent, and they just kept coming. We started off with some bruschetta, pretty decent portion and it was quite good. For an entree we split the capellini shrimp, a basic dish but pretty tasty, just shrimp, artichoke hearts and sundried tomatoes in a white wine sauce.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nAll told, our bill for two only came out to a little of $20, which was pretty shocking given the great service and generous portions, not to mention the beach location.    Best Italian Restaurant in LA. I first discovered C&amp;O in 1993 and it immediately became my favorite restaurant in LA. The food is just as good now as it was back then: gigantic portions, creative, well-flavored dishes, remarkably reasonable prices. The garlic knots are free and stinky and delicious, and the nightly &quot;That's Amore&quot; singalong is great fun. My favorite meal starts with the spinach-prosciutto-pine nut salad; the gnocchi, or the capellini shrimp, or the penne with sausage are all winner entrees (though it's hard to get a bad one!); and the dolci are all top rate.<br />\r\n<br />\r\nThe only knock I have is its popularity among the Tragically Hip Crowd. It's no longer the sweet neighborhood bistro from 1993, now you have to wait for a table and sit in an incredibly noisy dining room if you want a delicious dinner. However, the food really makes the wait worthwhile.\n                                                            <div class=\"clear\"></div>\n                </div>\n            \n            \n
